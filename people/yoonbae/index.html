Spatial or spatio-temporal data are gaining increasing interest as we see such data in various fields. I have been broadly studying statistical methodologies that can be used to analyze spatial data within the disciplines of spatial statistics and environmental epidemiology. With the help of prominent researchers at Seoul National University, I am developing a distinctive research career in three major branches that consist of Fourier spectral analysis, air pollution epidemiology, and Bayesian Markov chain Monte Carlo modeling. I will continue study further in this direction by working as a postdoctoral fellow under the supervision of Dr. Zhengyuan Zhu, Iowa state university, starting from August, 2021.
